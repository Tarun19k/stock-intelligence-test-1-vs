## 1. Brand Positioning Statement

AlphaVeda is a personal portfolio analytics platform for Indian retail investors who want to move beyond gut-feel decisions and into data-driven market understanding. By synthesising technical signals, fundamental indicators, and macro-level patterns across NSE and BSE-listed equities, AlphaVeda surfaces the patterns in your portfolio and the market — giving you the analytical clarity to ask better questions and make more informed choices on your own. AlphaVeda is an analytics tool, not an advisor; it illuminates the data, you drive the decision.

## 3. Brand Personality

### Attribute 1: Precise

The language of AlphaVeda is exact. Data is presented with source, timeframe, and methodology visible. Nothing is vague.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "This stock's 52-week RSI trend shows sustained momentum above 60 across 8 of the last 12 months." | "This stock looks strong right now." |
| "Price-to-earnings ratio: 24.3x vs. sector median 18.7x — 30% premium to peers." | "Expensive compared to others in the sector." |

---

### Attribute 2: Calm

AlphaVeda never alarms, hypes, or reacts. The tone is the opposite of financial Twitter. The market moves; AlphaVeda holds steady.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Volatility in this segment is elevated relative to 90-day baseline. Your portfolio exposure here is 18%." | "ALERT: Your portfolio is at risk! Markets crashing!" |
| "This pattern has preceded corrections in 3 of the last 5 comparable cycles." | "Sell everything before it's too late." |

---

### Attribute 3: Deeply Indian

AlphaVeda speaks to the Indian retail investor — NSE/BSE tickers, sector classifications by SEBI, Nifty/Sensex as benchmark defaults, rupee denomination throughout. No US-market defaults, no generic "portfolio" language that ignores Indian market realities (circuit filters, T+1 settlement, F&O rollover cycles).

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Benchmark: Nifty 500 TRI (total returns index, dividends included)." | "Benchmark: S&P 500 equivalent." |
| "Your ELSS allocation currently sits at ₹1.6L — within 80C limit." | "Your tax-advantaged allocation is X." |

---

### Attribute 4: Empowering, Not Advisory

AlphaVeda puts analytical power in the user's hands. It never tells you what to do. It tells you what the data says, so you can decide.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Three technical indicators are aligned positively for this ticker as of today's close." | "This is a buy signal." |
| "Your portfolio's correlation to Bank Nifty has increased from 0.42 to 0.71 over 6 months." | "Reduce your banking sector exposure." |

---

### Attribute 5: Rooted in Knowledge (Veda)

The Veda dimension is not decorative. AlphaVeda treats market intelligence as a discipline — a structured body of knowledge that compounds over time, like learning. The product should feel like gaining fluency, not just getting an alert.

| Do | Don't |
|----|-------|
| "Your portfolio's pattern recognition score has improved as your historical data deepens." | "Premium users get more signals." |
| Explain the methodology behind every metric in plain language. | Hide how a score is calculated behind a paywall label. |

## 4. Tone of Voice

### Principle 1: Say what the data shows — not what it means for the user's decision

AlphaVeda describes; the user decides. The moment the product crosses into recommendation territory, it crosses a SEBI line and loses the user's trust in the data itself.

| AlphaVeda says | AlphaVeda does not say |
|----------------|------------------------|
| "Price momentum (6-month) is in the top quartile of this index." | "Now is a good time to add to this position." |
| "Promoter holding has declined 4.2% in the last two quarters." | "Watch out — insiders may be exiting." |

---

### Principle 2: Plain language over jargon, but never dumbed down

The target user knows what PE ratio means. They do not need a glossary for basics. AlphaVeda uses precise financial terms with brief inline context for anything non-standard — it treats the user as a capable adult who is still learning.

| AlphaVeda says | AlphaVeda does not say |
|----------------|------------------------|
| "EV/EBITDA: 11.2x — a common valuation multiple for capital-intensive businesses." | "Enterprise Value to EBITDA multiple equals 11.2." |
| "Debt-to-equity ratio: 0.8 (sector range: 0.3–1.4)." | "D/E ratio is moderate. Be careful." |

---

### Principle 3: Confident but never certain

Markets are uncertain. AlphaVeda reflects that. Probabilistic framing, historical comparisons, and "as of" timestamps are standard. Absolute claims are never made.

| AlphaVeda says | AlphaVeda does not say |
|----------------|------------------------|
| "In 7 of the last 10 comparable macro environments, this sector saw compressed margins for 2–3 quarters." | "Margins will compress this quarter." |
| "Pattern resembles Q3 FY22 setup — context differs, but overlap is notable." | "This is exactly what happened in 2022." |

## 5. Visual Identity Direction

### Color Palette

**Primary Colors**

| Role | Name | Hex | Rationale |
|------|------|-----|-----------|

**Secondary Colors**

**Accent Colors**

| Role | Name | Hex | Rationale |
|------|------|-----|-----------|
| Positive / Up | Vedic Green | `#4A8C5C` | Portfolio gains, positive signals. Deliberately muted — not neon. Calm, not celebratory. |
| Negative / Down | Terracotta | `#C0503A` | Drawdowns, risk flags. Warm red — Indian palette, not alarming Western red. |
| Neutral / Mid | Mid Warm Gray | `#9B9490` | Secondary text, borders, inactive states. |

**Usage ratios (approximate):**
- Deep Indigo + Warm Ivory: 70% of any surface
- Saffron Gold: max 15% (hero accents, CTAs, selected states)
- Slate Teal: 10% (charts, data layers)
- Vedic Green / Terracotta: signal use only (never decorative)

---

### Typography

**Heading Font: Fraunces**  
Optical serif with warmth and character. Evokes the wisdom of the Veda half of the name. Distinct from every competitor in the space (all use geometric sans or system defaults). Use for product name, section titles, key metrics at large size.  
Fallback: Georgia, serif.

**Body / Data Font: DM Mono** (for data) + **Inter** (for prose)  
DM Mono at 13–14px for all numerical data, ticker symbols, timestamps, and table cells. This signals precision and separates data from narrative. Inter for all prose — UI copy, tooltips, onboarding, marketing copy. Clean, legible, neutral.  
Fallbacks: Courier New (data), system-ui (prose).

---

### Logo Concept Directions

**Concept A: The Yantra Mark**  
A simplified geometric form inspired by the Sri Yantra — concentric triangles, but reduced to 2–3 clean lines forming a diamond/upward-pointing triangle with a subtle internal structure. The triangle points upward (alpha, growth, above benchmark). The internal geometry nods to Vedic mathematics without being religious. Wordmark in Fraunces sits to the right. Works well at small sizes as an icon.

**Concept B: The Alpha-Akshara**  
A custom letterform that merges the Greek alpha symbol (α) with the Devanagari stroke style — thick-thin contrast, a slight calligraphic curve at the terminal. The result is a mark that reads as both "α" and as something rooted in Indian script tradition. Single-color; scales from favicon to billboard. Most distinctive of the three options.

**Concept C: The Constellation Mark**  
Five dots arranged in an asymmetric pattern (mimicking a star cluster), connected by thin lines — representing signal nodes in a market intelligence network. Clean, modern, tech-adjacent. The dots can animate in the digital product (signals appearing one by one). Less India-specific but the most universally legible at small sizes. Works well if the product plans international expansion.

**Recommended direction:** Concept A or B. Concept A for safer launch (geometric, timeless); Concept B for stronger differentiation if a custom typeface budget is available.

## 6. SEBI-Compliant Marketing Language Guide

### Context

SEBI's Research Analyst Regulations (2014) and Investment Adviser Regulations (2013) regulate entities that provide investment advice or research recommendations for consideration. AlphaVeda must be positioned clearly as an analytics and data tool — not a registered research analyst or investment adviser.

The rule of thumb: **describe the data, never prescribe the action.**

---

### Approved Phrases (use freely)

| Category | Approved Language |
|----------|-------------------|
| Product description | "Portfolio analytics platform", "market data visualisation", "pattern recognition tool", "data-driven portfolio monitoring", "equity analytics dashboard" |
| Signal language | "Technical indicator reading", "momentum signal", "historical pattern match", "statistical overlap with prior periods", "data point to explore" |
| Performance language | "Portfolio return vs. benchmark index (informational)", "historical volatility reading", "drawdown observation", "allocation concentration metric" |
| User empowerment | "Understand your portfolio better", "surface the data behind your holdings", "analytical clarity for your own research", "make more informed choices" |
| Disclaimer anchors | "For informational and analytical purposes only", "not investment advice", "past patterns are not predictive of future performance", "consult a SEBI-registered advisor for personalised guidance" |

---

### Prohibited Phrases (never use)

| Category | Prohibited Language | Risk |
|----------|--------------------|----|
| Recommendations | "Buy", "sell", "hold", "add to your position", "exit now", "reduce exposure" | Investment advice — requires SEBI RA registration |
| Return promises | "Guaranteed returns", "assured alpha", "beat the market with AlphaVeda", "generate X% returns" | Misleading advertising, SEBI violation |
| Price targets | "Target price: ₹XXX", "upside of X%", "downside risk of X%" | Research report — requires SEBI RA registration |
| Urgency / FOMO | "Act now before it's too late", "limited-time opportunity", "don't miss this stock" | Manipulative framing, advertising standards violation |
| Advisory framing | "AlphaVeda recommends", "our top picks", "stock of the week", "curated portfolio" | Advisory service — requires SEBI IA registration |
| Certification claims | "Endorsed by SEBI", "SEBI-approved signals", "certified analytics" | False claim — SEBI does not certify analytics tools |

---

### Mandatory Disclaimer (to appear on all marketing surfaces and in-product)

> AlphaVeda is an analytics and data visualisation tool for informational purposes only. It does not provide investment advice, research recommendations, or portfolio management services. All data displayed is sourced from publicly available market feeds and is not independently verified. Past market patterns and portfolio analytics do not guarantee future performance. Users should consult a SEBI-registered Investment Adviser or Research Analyst before making any investment decisions.

## 7. Target Persona

### Primary Persona: "The Systematic Self-Investor"

**Name (illustrative):** Arjun, 34  
**Location:** Bengaluru / Mumbai / Pune  
**Occupation:** Mid-senior professional (tech, consulting, finance-adjacent) with household income ₹25L–₹80L/year  
**Investment profile:** 3–8 years of equity investing experience. Direct equity portfolio of ₹5L–₹30L. Uses Zerodha or Groww for execution. Has a demat account, knows basic fundamentals, has tried Screener.in at least once.

**What they want:**
- To understand *why* their portfolio is performing the way it is — not just the P&L number
- A single view that connects technical momentum, fundamental health, and their actual holdings
- To feel like they are making decisions based on evidence, not tips or instinct
- Something that makes them smarter over time, not just gives them alerts

**What they fear:**
- Making a significant loss they "should have seen coming" if they'd just looked at the data properly
- Falling for a hot tip (social media, WhatsApp group, business news channel) and losing capital
- Being too late — buying at peak, selling at trough — because they didn't have the right signals in front of them
- That sophisticated analytics tools are only for HNIs or institutional desks — not for them

**Their current workaround:**
- Tabs open: Screener.in (fundamentals), TradingView (charts), Moneycontrol (news), Zerodha Kite (positions) — but nothing connects them
- Spends 2–4 hours on weekends manually reconciling information across tools
- Knows this is inefficient; suspects a better system exists but hasn't found it

**Price sensitivity:**
- Happy to pay ₹500–₹1,500/month for something that saves meaningful research time and improves confidence in their decisions
- Will not pay for something that feels like "another screener" — needs to perceive clear synthesis value

## 8. Content Pillars

### Pillar 1: Market Intelligence Explained

*"Understand what the market is telling you — not what pundits are saying."*

Content that demystifies signals, indicators, and macro patterns for the self-directed retail investor. No tips. Pure education around how to read data. Builds authority without triggering SEBI advisory lines.

**Format examples:** Explainer threads (LinkedIn/X), short video breakdowns (YouTube), "Signal of the Week" newsletter section (what the indicator shows, not what to do with it), deep-dive blog posts on technical + fundamental synthesis.

**Sample topics:**
- "What does a P/E ratio at 40x actually mean for your risk?" (explainer, not recommendation)
- "How to read a Nifty 50 sector rotation chart — and what it tells you about your own allocation"
- "RSI divergence: what the chart is saying vs. what people think it's saying"

---

### Pillar 2: Portfolio Self-Awareness

*"Most investors know their returns. Few understand their portfolio."*

Content focused on portfolio construction, concentration risk, benchmark calibration, and the analytical hygiene of managing your own equity book. Uniquely AlphaVeda — this is where synthesis across signals becomes visible.

**Format examples:** Portfolio teardown case studies (anonymised), "Portfolio Metrics You Should Be Tracking" guides, checklists, data literacy frameworks for retail investors.

**Sample topics:**
- "The 5 metrics Screener.in doesn't show you — and why they matter"
- "How concentrated is too concentrated? Sector exposure and correlation in Indian retail portfolios"
- "Your portfolio vs. Nifty 500 TRI: how to measure alpha correctly"

---

### Pillar 3: Indian Market Context

*"Indian markets have their own logic. Your analytics should too."*

Content that speaks specifically to NSE/BSE dynamics — circuit filters, SEBI policy impacts, FII/DII flows, F&O expiry effects, GST + earnings seasonality, Budget cycle positioning. This pillar differentiates AlphaVeda from generic global tools and builds the deeply-Indian brand attribute.

**Format examples:** Monthly market context reports (data-only, no recommendations), FII flow analysis explainers, sector-specific India context primers, "This month's macro backdrop" newsletter anchor.

**Sample topics:**
- "FII vs. DII flow divergence in June 2026 — what the data shows"
- "Budget cycle patterns in Indian equities: 15 years of sector-level data"
- "How F&O expiry week affects your equity holdings — the numbers"
